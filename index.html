What to expect from each filter?

Image Filter

Brightness - defined as the amount of energy output by a source of light relative to the source we are comparing it to.
Contrast - difference between maximum and minimum pixel intensity in an image.
Saturation - strength or purity of the color and represents the amount of gray in proportion to the hue.
Vibrance - A smart-tool which increases the intensity of the more muted colors, leaving saturated colors alone,also prevents skin tones from becoming overly saturated and unnatural.
Exposure - It is the amount of light per unit area reaching a frame of photographic film or the surface of an electronic image sensor, as determined by shutter speed, lens aperture, and scene luminance.
Noise - A random variation of brightness or color information in images.
Sharpness - Refers to an image's overall clarity in terms of both focus and contrast.
Blur - Make indistinct and hazy in outline or appearance.
